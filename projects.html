Revere inverse is the process by which we can infer cognitive functions within the brain using brain imaging data. Where forward inference is defined by the probability of observing brain activity given the mental state under investigation, reverse inference is the probability of a particular mental state given the observed brain activity.

Here, I explored the potential of using ML models to predict diagnosis of new patients using neuroimaging data of individuals that were diagnosed with Schizophrenia and individuals that were not.

Traffic Modeling
1.

A cellular automata model using the Intelligent Driver Model (IDM) updating rules with the implementation of static and dynamic traffic signals.

A static traffic signal has a fixed signal cycle after which the light changes, e.g., a light change every 10 seconds. While a dynamic signal strategy is vehicle-density dependent and optimizes traffic flow by changing the signal of the road pair that has more vehicles compared to the other. The dynamic signal strategy was shown to significantly decrease vehicle density over time and increase average traffic flow as compared to a static signal model.

Predicting Air quality
1.

I used different kinds of Bayesian linear regression models to analyze correlational data of No2 and Co levels in Argentina.

  • Normal distribution: a normal likelihood, the mean of which is informed by a linear model.
  • Student T distribution: a student T distribution likelihood, with heavier tails to accommodate outliers
  • Outlier classifier distribution: a normal likelihood, with every datum being assigned as either an outlier or not.

I compared the models using posterior regression means and Pareto-Smoothed Importance Sampling (PSIS) cross-validation and found that the Outlier classifier model performed the best, in and out of sample.
